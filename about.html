Welcome to Our School

REDWOOD started in the summer (Raining Season) of August 2014. In September of that year we kicked off the session with 18 teaching and non-teaching staff plus 22 children who occupied all the classes from crèche to primary 6. In other words, we had at least a child in every class. We have grown over the years in terms of staff strength, number of children, content, facilities and infrastructure. We continue in the spirit of excellence with our vision before us to produce creative, innovative and enthusiastic lifelong learners.

We love what we do and pledge to do it with integrity. We respect the dignity and worth of every child in our care and inculcate same in them. We believe that all children can be nurtured to achieve their full potentials within our school, when we love them unconditionally and challenge them to be their best. We expect our students to exhibit confidence, be resilient and strive for excellence every where they go.

Our Unique Features

We have adopted a unique blend of British, Montessori and our very own Nigerian curriculum. A couple of the learning areas are competency based. Here, children show what they know from sub-skill to sub-skill until the concept that is being taught is fully mastered before moving on to the next. This is done Irrespective of the grade and age. There are no gaps and no child is truly left behind.

Every lesson in our school is laced with meaningful activities where children are provided with ample opportunities to be involved in the process of learning using hands-on materials. We adopt the maxim, To learn it! Do it!

Research has shown that children learn best when they are active participants of their own learning rather than passive spectators. We believe that children are competent co-creators of their own knowledge. Hence, we create playful learning experiences that embrace their natural curiosity, laying a solid foundation for deep learning to occur

Often , the resultant effect is that our learners are able to show what they know in real and varied life situations.

To prepare our children for the complexities and uncertainties of the 21st century, we have inculcated in our present curricular special inter-disciplinary skills world organisations and multi-industry leader’s term “21st century skills”.

Chief among them are: Effective communication and collaboration, creativity, critical thinking and problem solving. Others include; initiative, decision making, negotiation, citizenship, financial and digital literacy.

We do not know how different life would be 20 to 30 years from now but we know that many jobs would be wiped off the list of employable jobs. Th It has been established that the above skill- set would set the children’s future learning, life and carrier on the path of success.

Play is a child’s work, so says Maria-Montessori the founder of the world-acclaimed Montessori teaching method.

Fun filled activities are a hallmark of an education in Redwood Academy. When children are having fun, learning becomes emotional and unforgettable, literally. So while we learn the serious stuff, we include different types of play, in the midst of the jiggling the love for learning and for the teacher is heightened and voila! transformation occurs.

Play based learning is multisensory and fulfils the interest of the predominant learning styles of our learners found in any class: the auditory, visual, tactile and kinaesthetic learners. No child is left behind!

We live in a technologically driven world where the absence of digital skills will greatly mar an individual’s progress and effectiveness irrespective of the discipline. This is why we have chosen to engage our children in digital skills far beyond the recommendations from the National curriculum.

Our children are taught to use applications to create projects or presentations, solve problems and increase their learning ability.

Our teachers utilize technology in the teaching and learning process as well as in assessing what children know and are able to do.

One way in which this is done is having many of our lessons gamified electronically. Children absolutely love games because it provides a no-pressure, fun environment where they are allowed to make their mistakes in peace, where they are permitted to try over and over again without being judged, until they fully master the concept!

The COVID-19 lock down warranted by the pandemic, actually brought about a dramatic turn in the mode and method of learning. Schools and homes came to terms with learning not only in-person but in virtual spaces.

Redwood has applied that in many ways as well as in our field trips. We travel round the world virtually, looking at and learning from interesting artefacts, land forms, major cities, urban and rural area’s . We learn about the people, their language, culture and customs.

Our virtual trips have elicited as much fun as the live field trips.

At Redwood, we take the continuous development of our teachers very seriously because Teachers are the engine room of a school and play a very important role even beyond the school to national development. We know that the quality of teachers is directly related to the quality of the outcomes of pupils.

Our teachers therefore, undergo weekly and periodic continuous professional development sessions, preparing them for the onerous task of teaching.
